# Projection and Angles

## Scalar Projection
The length of the shadow $\mathbf{u}$ casts onto $\mathbf{v}$ is:
$$\text{comp}_{\mathbf{v}} \mathbf{u} = \frac{\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{v}}{\|\mathbf{v}\|}$$

## Vector Projection
This is the actual vector that lies on the line of $\mathbf{v}$ and represents the "shadow":
$$\text{proj}_{\mathbf{v}} \mathbf{u} = \left( \frac{\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{v}}{\|\mathbf{v}\|^2} \right) \mathbf{v}$$

## Finding the Angle
If you have the coordinates of two vectors but don't know the angle, you rearrange the dot product:
$$\cos(\theta) = \frac{\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{v}}{\|\mathbf{u}\| \|\mathbf{v}\|}$$
