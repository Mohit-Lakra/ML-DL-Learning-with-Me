# Basis and Dimension

## What is a Basis?
A set of vectors $\mathcal{B} = \{\mathbf{b}_1, \dots, \mathbf{b}_n\}$ is a basis for a vector space $V$ if:
1. The vectors are linearly independent.
2. The vectors span V.

A basis is a "minimal spanning set." It has enough vectors to cover the whole space, but no extras.

Example: The standard basis for $\mathbb{R}^2$ is $\mathbf{i} = [1, 0]$ and $\mathbf{j} = [0, 1]$. Any vector $[x, y]$ can be written as $x\mathbf{i} + y\mathbf{j}$, and you can't build $\mathbf{i}$ from $\mathbf{j}$.

## What is Dimension?
The dimension of a vector space is simply the number of vectors in its basis.
- If a subspace is a plane, its dimension is 2.
- If it's a line, its dimension is 1.
